Why are they Rivals?

Eddie Brock, first develops his hatred of Spider-Man during Spider-Man’s Sin-Eater storyline, where Spider-Man discovers the masked gunman Sin-Eater who killed his friend, police captain Jean DeWolff, is actually fellow police officer Stan Carter. While Brock doesn’t appear in the original comic book story, it’s later revealed that Brock (who in the comics is a reporter like in the Venom film) wrote a series of articles claiming another man, Emil Gregg, was the Sin-Eater. When Spider-Man exposes the real Sin-Eater, Brock’s journalistic career is ruined and he begins blaming Spider-Man for ruining his life.

Spiderman

Peter Parker was an orphaned teenage boy who lived in Queens, NY with his Aunt May and Uncle Ben. He was a high school student and a prodigy that had a passion for science. While on a class field trip he was bitten by a genetically enhanced spider.

Peter Parker vows to not use his powers for his own gain after the death of his Uncle Ben. To start the series he mainly protects New York later down the line joins the Avengers to save Earth.

Spiderman has a wide variety of powers they are: communication with spiders, super strength, super healing, night vision, super agility and speed, wall-crawling, spidey sense and web slinging.

Venom

The Origin History of Venom begins with the creature that would become Venom being born to a race of extra-terrestrial symbiotes which lived by possessing the bodies of other life forms. These parasites would empower their victims with superior physical abilities at the cost of fatally draining them of adrenaline.

Eddie Brock was the first person to become Venom and by far my favorite Venom. Eddie was a reporter for The Daily Globe before it comes to light that he had fabricated a story revealing the identity of the Sin-Eater. Shortly after the story was published Spider-Man pursued and captured the real Sin-Eater disgracing Brock as a news reporter and costing him his job and then his wife.

Venoms Powers include : Shape Shifting , Strength , Regeneration and Agility

Black Panther

After the death of his father at the hands of the villainous Ulysses Klaw, T’Challa claimed the throne as well as the mantle of the Black Panther. Upon becoming the Black Panther, T’Challa was exposed to a mystical herb that enhanced his strength and agility to near-superhuman levels.

T’Challa decided his powers would be put to best use in the service of all humanity.

Killmonger

An ex navy seal member of the United States, he had the highest count of confirmed kills amongst everyone in his unit.

Eventually, Killmonger was recruited into a Joint Special Operations Command ghost unit associated with the CIA as a highly skilled black ops mercenary. Operating within the JSOC unit, he went off the grid in order to commit assassinations and take down foreign governments on behalf of the United States. As the highly-skilled soldier, Stevens was responsible for eliminating dozens of hostiles, cutting his skin with tribal Crocodile Scarring, with one for each kill. Due to his ruthlessness, while serving within the military, thus earning himself the nickname “Killmonger”.

Why are they Rivals?

Even with them being family members, Killmonger hates Black Panther and Wakanda. Killmonger's father was killed by his brother, the previous king of Wakanda (Black Panther's Father), then he was abandoned in Harlem, and since then he has seeked revenge on Wakanda.

When Killmonger becomes an adult, he goes into Wakanda and challenges Black Panther for the throne. He wanted to become the king of Wakanda, to use the vibranium technology that Wakanda uses to help all the black communities around the world that had been oppressed for hundreds of years. But his main goal was to get revenge for his father.

Black Panther would later be defeated by Killmonger in their duel for the throne. Killmonger throws Black Panther off the side of the waterfall and becomes the new king of Wakanda.

Though everyone presumed that Black Panther was dead, he would later come back and kill Killmonger, reclaiming the throne of Wakanda.

Why are they Rivals?

Though they are friends, Professor X and Magneto have differing opinions on how to integrate in the world as Mutants.

Professor X wants Mutants to use their special abilities to help the world and humans, so both species can be cordial and thrive in the same environment.

Magneto on the otherhand, has a distrust with humans and wants to protect his own people by using their mutant abilities to enhance their own lives and achieving as much as possible, by any means necessary.

Therefore, these friends/foes clash over their ideas often. Professor X along with the X-Men, try to stop Magneto and his band of mutants from wreaking havoc amongst humans.

Professor X

Charles Xavier or Professor X is a mutant, leader of the X-Men, and founder of the Xavier School for Gifted Youngsters. He is a scientific genius and a powerful telepath.

Professor X trains and teaches mutants how to deal with their abilities and how to live in a world that fears them.

His powers include: scanning the minds of people for information, sense nearby mutants, mind control, make people fall asleep, manipulate the memories of others.

Magneto

Erik Lehnsherr or Magneto is a mutant that can control magnetic fields and metallic objects. Originally a founder of the X-Men, he left to form the Brotherhood of Mutants, a mutant based terrorist organization that fights for mutant supremacy over humans.

In 1944, he was separated from his parents in the Auschwitz concentration camp. Here, he witnessed his mother’s murder and in a fit of rage, Magneto exposed his powers to a mutant Nazi collaborator. The Nazis began torturing and experimenting on him to expand his magnetic powers. Seeing mankind’s cruelty, he is devoted to protecting his kind, the Mutants.

His powers include: Generating and manipulating all forms of magnetism, Metal manipulation, Fly, Sense metal in his environment
